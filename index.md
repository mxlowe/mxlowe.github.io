<h3>About</h3><p> 
    		Matthew X. Lowe is a research scientist with over 10 years of experience researching factors that influence human behavior and brain function. He is currently a Scientific Advisor for Poppy Life Care, a  501(c)(3) non-profit organization promoting education and wellness for individuals and families affected by Autism Spectrum Disorder, depression, anxiety, and Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder. Previously, he was a Director of Research at Stanford University, a postdoctoral associate at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), and a visiting research fellow at Harvard Medical School and Brigham and Women's Hospital. Matthew obtained his PhD from the University of Toronto, and his bachelor's degree from the University of Cape Town.<\p>  
		
<p>Outside of his work, Matthew is an avid adventurer. He has trekked through the Arctic Circle and climbed to the peak of the highest mountains in South America (Mt. Aconcagua), Europe (Mt. Elbrus), Africa (Mt. Kilimanjaro), and his home country, South Africa (Mafadi).</p>

<p> Matthew lives with his partner, Hadley, and their dog, Kansas, in San Francisco, California.</p>
